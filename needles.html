Needle Anatomy & Classification

Understanding needle components and design principles

Point

The sharp tip that penetrates tissue. Design varies based on tissue type and surgical application.

Body

The main portion grasped by needle holder. Determines curvature and handling characteristics.

Swage

The attachment point where suture material is connected to the needle. Creates smooth transition.

Needle Handling Techniques

Best practices for safe and effective needle use

Needle Grasping

Grasp needle at the junction of anterior 2/3 and posterior 1/3. This provides optimal control and prevents bending.

Penetration Angle

Enter tissue at 90Â° angle for optimal wound edge eversion and minimal tissue trauma.

Needle Path

Follow the needle's natural curve. Avoid forcing or straightening the needle during passage.

Tissue Control

Use forceps to stabilize tissue without crushing. Support wound edges during needle passage.

Depth Control

Maintain consistent depth on both sides of wound. Depth should equal tissue thickness.

Safety Considerations

Always pass needles away from yourself and assistants. Use needle holders, not fingers.
